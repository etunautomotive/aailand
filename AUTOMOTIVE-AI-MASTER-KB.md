## Executive Overview

Automotive AI (AAI) is a modern, AI-native customer relationship management platform built exclusively for car dealerships. It is a B2B SaaS product sold to dealerships as a subscription with pricing tied to plan tier and usage.

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Dealerships on platform | 100+ |
| Monthly recurring revenue | $50K |
| Native integrations | 11 |

Automotive AI replaces the fragmented combination of spreadsheets, generic CRMs, and disconnected messaging apps that most dealerships cobble together — with a single platform purpose-built around automotive sales workflows. Unlike generic CRMs adapted for automotive, every feature — from how leads are captured to how follow-ups are sent to how campaigns are measured — is designed around how dealerships actually sell.

The platform is built by former top-performing automotive salespeople who lived the grind on the sales floor. This is not a tech company that studied the industry from the outside — it is a team of closers who learned tech to solve problems they experienced firsthand.

## The Problem

Car dealerships face a specific set of sales challenges that off-the-shelf CRMs handle poorly:

### Leads Fall Through the Cracks
Inquiries arrive from websites, Facebook, phone, and email simultaneously. Without a unified inbox and automated follow-up, salespeople miss leads or respond too slowly — and in automotive, speed-to-lead is directly correlated with close rate.

### Follow-Up Is Person-Dependent
Most dealerships rely on individual salespeople to follow up manually. Some are disciplined; most aren't. There is no system enforcing a consistent outreach cadence, so deals die from inaction rather than lack of interest.

### Marketing Spend Has No Attribution
Dealers invest heavily in Facebook ads, Google, and third-party listing sites — but have no way to tie a specific campaign to a closed deal. Budget decisions are made on gut feel rather than data.

### Teams Operate in Silos
Sales reps, team leads, and managers have no shared visibility into pipeline status, rep activity, or which conversations are warm versus cold. Managers can't coach what they can't see.

### Disjointed Tech Stacks
Dealerships juggle multiple disconnected tools — CRM, manual texting, spreadsheets, call logs, sticky notes, DealerTrack, email, WhatsApp, Slack — creating disorganization, lost opportunities, overwhelmed workflows, and enormous time waste per deal.

### Inconsistent Results and Turnover
Results depend entirely on individual effort, not systems. Staff turnover means constantly retraining, and when strong performers leave, their scripts, follow-up cadences, and relationship knowledge walk out the door. New hires start from scratch.

## Core Features & Capabilities

### 1. Lead & Contact Management

The central database for every prospect and customer.

- Centralized contact database with full history and activity tracking
- Leads flow in automatically from web forms, email, Facebook, Google Sheets, DMS systems, and webhooks — no manual entry
- Bulk contact import from CSV/Excel with automatic duplicate detection
- Lead source tracking with cost-per-lead and ROI analysis
- Visual sales pipelines with drag-and-drop stage management
- Contact notes and annotations for team collaboration
- Smart auto-segmentation of contacts based on behavior and attributes
- Full activity history and notes per contact

### 2. Multi-Channel Communication

All customer communication happens inside one inbox — no app switching.

- **SMS** — Send and receive text messages directly from the CRM
- **AI Voice Calling** — Automated outbound calls powered by AI with call recording and transcription
- **Facebook Messenger** — Respond to customers on Messenger without leaving the platform
- **Webchat** — Embeddable chat widget for dealership websites
- **Email** — Send emails and automatically capture leads from Gmail inboxes
- **SMS & Email Templates** — Reusable templates with personalization variables for consistent messaging across the team

### 3. AI-Powered Intelligence

AI runs through the entire platform — not bolted on.

- **AI-generated message responses** with customizable tone and persona
- **Credit report analysis** — AI reads and summarizes credit reports instantly
- **Sentiment analysis** to classify conversations as warm or cold
- **Automatic field extraction** — AI pulls contact details from incoming messages
- **Lead scoring and qualification** powered by AI
- **Support for multiple AI providers** (Claude, Gemini, GPT) with per-dealership configuration
- **AI usage dashboard** to monitor consumption and costs (tokens and cost breakdowns by model)

The AI is trained on proven sales processes developed by the founding team during their time as top-performing automotive salespeople. It communicates like a human — with natural language patterns, appropriate tone matching, and chameleon tactics that adapt to the customer's communication style. The AI can build enough trust to collect sensitive information like SIN numbers, driver's licenses, home addresses, and paystubs via text without the customer knowing they are interacting with AI.

### 4. Campaign Management

Run targeted outreach to specific contact segments.

- Create SMS and calling campaigns targeting specific contact segments
- Schedule campaigns with timezone-aware delivery windows
- Flexible distribution: equal, custom, or progressive release over time
- Slot-based scheduling — half-hourly, hourly, daily, or weekly batches
- Daily SMS quota management tied to dealership plans
- Real-time campaign status tracking (scheduled, in-progress, sent, failed)
- Campaign performance analytics with success/failure breakdowns and response rates
- Pre-built marketplace campaign templates so teams don't start from blank

### 5. Automation & Workflows

Event-driven automations eliminate manual repetitive work.

- **Triggers:** new lead arrival, status change, incoming message
- **Actions:** send SMS, fire webhooks, create notifications, update contact fields, assign to reps
- Follow-up sequences with configurable delays and conditions
- Scheduled message automation for timed outreach
- Webhook integrations with automatic retry on failure
- Marketplace of pre-built automation templates ready to import and use

### 6. Dashboard & Reporting

Role-based views ensure everyone sees relevant metrics.

- Role-based dashboards for Admin, Team Lead, and Sales Rep
- Contact performance: new leads, contacted, closed, lost, in-progress
- Campaign analytics: messages sent, success rates, response rates
- Call metrics: total calls, duration, answered vs. missed
- Lead source ROI: track which sources deliver the best return
- Date range filtering and period-over-period comparisons
- AI usage metrics: token consumption, cost breakdowns by model

### 7. Calendar & Appointments

- Two-way Google Calendar sync
- Create calendar events directly from the CRM
- Availability-based scheduling for appointments
- Appointment booking tied to campaigns and follow-ups

### 8. Document Management

- Upload and store documents against contacts
- Customizable coversheets with dealership-specific fields
- PDF generation with dynamic field mapping
- AI-powered invoice and document processing via Google Document AI
- Secure file storage with access controls

### 9. Credit Applications

- Integrated credit application processing
- AI-powered credit report reading and summarization
- Automatic PII sanitization for compliance
- Co-applicant support
- Field mapping between credit systems and CRM data

### 10. Notifications

- In-app real-time notifications
- Mobile push notifications (Firebase for iOS and Android)
- Browser push notifications
- Customizable notification sounds and preferences
- Per-user notification type controls

### 11. Team & User Management

- Team hierarchy with leads and members
- Role-based access: Superadmin, Admin, Team Lead, Sales Rep
- User assignment to contacts and campaigns
- Account enable/disable controls
- Team-scoped visibility — reps see their own data, leads see their team

### 12. Dealership Configuration

- Multi-dealership support with complete data isolation
- Per-dealership settings: timezone, AI model, templates, pipelines
- Dealership snapshots for point-in-time configuration capture
- Custom document types and coversheet layouts

### 13. Billing & Subscriptions

- SMS quotas tied to subscription plans

## Platform Architecture

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Multi-Tenant** | Each dealership operates in complete isolation. Config, pipelines, templates, and AI model are all per-dealership. No data bleeds between accounts. |
| **AI-Native** | AI runs through communication, analysis, and automation. Choose Claude, Gemini, or GPT per dealership. |
| **Real-Time** | Live notifications via in-app, browser push, and Firebase mobile push across the full team. |
| **Role-Based** | Superadmin → Admin → Team Lead → Rep, with scoped data visibility at each level. Granular access control ensures everyone sees exactly what they need. |
| **Mobile-Ready** | Progressive web app with push notifications and offline support — no native install required. |
| **Automation-First** | Event-driven workflows and follow-up sequences are central, not add-ons. Marketplace gives a starting point. |

## Brand Foundation

### Origin Story

Automotive AI was not built in a lab or designed by investors. It was forged on the sales floor by a team of closers who lived every problem the product now solves.

The founding team — Wes, Josh, Morgan, and Aneesh — all have roots in small-town Alberta. Wes and Josh played football for the same high school team, wore the same number, competed for the same positions, and both went on to play at the university level. Morgan grew up as a blue-collar worker with an extreme work ethic and a willingness to learn.

Wes's entry into automotive came through a cold-calling job where he was offered $4,000/month to start the next day. He took it. Over the next nine months, he relentlessly applied the same discipline from sports to mastering sales. Josh, still playing football at the time, started delivering vehicles and eventually felt the energy of the sales operation and made the leap. Morgan was working for CN Rail — a top producer making great money but missing freedom. After staying with Wes and seeing the opportunity, he spent three months memorizing the sales playbook and quit his railroad career.

The three lived together in a one-bedroom apartment, working 12-14 hour days mastering lead cultivation, closing, and process systemization. At their peak, they were doing $300K/month with young, inexperienced salespeople — by building systems, culture, and discipline rather than relying on individual talent.

They experienced firsthand every problem that dealerships face: inconsistent follow-up, repetitive manual tasks, databases full of untouched money, high turnover, disconnected tools duct-taped together, and 14-hour days just to stay average. They also lost $150K when poor money management ended their first venture.

After a period in corporate dealership roles — Josh in product development, Wes in management and systems, Morgan as a top closer — they reunited to build what they had always dreamed of: a CRM that would solve every issue they'd faced, now supercharged with AI that directly reflects the successful sales processes they ran and that delivered results.

Aneesh, the fourth founder, is the technical backbone — handling architecture, scaling, and backend infrastructure.

### Marketing Funnel Messaging

#### Top of Funnel (TOFU)
Grab attention. Create curiosity. Speak to the pain or opportunity. These messages don't require much context and should instantly resonate with someone who barely knows you exist. They're often bold, emotional, or promise a big outcome.

**Think:** "I feel that pain. Tell me more."
**Real-world examples:** Billboards, social ads, tradeshow booths

#### Middle of Funnel (MOFU)
Explain how you solve the problem. These messages take the TOFU idea and make it clear, concrete, and believable. MOFU messaging begins to outline what the product does and why it works — not in full detail, but enough to show: "This is real, this is relevant, this could help me."

**Think:** "I can picture how this would work for us."
**Real-world examples:** Website homepage, feature-specific webpages, email nurtures, blog or SEO-centric content

#### Bottom of Funnel (BOFU)
Provide proof, specifics, and confidence for decision-making. These messages speak to people who are comparing solutions or need the final push to justify the purchase. They typically include statistics, outcomes, credibility, and specific product mechanics.

**Think:** "It's obvious why we should choose this — let's buy."
**Real-world examples:** Decks, demos, pricing page, customer stories
